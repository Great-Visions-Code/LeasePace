# LeasePace 🚗📊

**Stay on pace with your lease mileage and avoid costly overage fees.**

LeasePace is a simple and practical iOS app designed to help drivers track their lease mileage usage, predict potential overage costs, and stay within their mileage limits throughout the life of their lease.

Many drivers don't realize they are over their mileage allowance until it's too late. LeasePace provides clear insights into daily mileage pacing, remaining allowance, and projected costs — helping users stay informed every mile of the way.

---

# 📱 Features

## Mileage Pace Tracking
See whether you're ahead, behind, or right on pace with your lease mileage.

## Overage Cost Estimation
Estimate how much extra miles could cost before your lease ends.

## Miles Remaining Calculator
Instantly view how many miles you have left in your lease.

## Daily Mileage Guidance
Know how many miles per day you should average to stay on track.

## Clean and Simple Design
Focused on clarity and usability — no clutter, just useful information.

# 🧠 How It Works

Users enter their lease details:

- Lease start date  
- Lease end date  
- Total miles allowed  
- Current mileage  
- Cost per mile over limit  

LeasePace calculates:

- Days remaining  
- Miles remaining  
- Allowed miles per day  
- Current mileage pace  
- Projected mileage at lease end  
- Estimated overage cost (if applicable)

---

# 🎯 Purpose

LeasePace was created to solve a common real-world problem: unexpected lease mileage penalties.

Mileage overage fees can quickly add up to hundreds or even thousands of dollars. LeasePace helps drivers stay aware of their usage patterns and adjust early, reducing the risk of surprise charges at lease turn-in.

This app focuses on delivering simple, actionable insights that empower users to make better driving decisions throughout their lease.

# 🧮 Core Calculations

LeasePace uses lease duration and mileage allowance to determine pacing metrics.

Key calculations include:

- Lease duration (in days)
- Miles allowed per day
- Miles used so far
- Expected mileage based on timeline
- Projected mileage at lease end
- Estimated overage cost

These calculations allow users to clearly see whether they are on pace or exceeding their lease limits.
